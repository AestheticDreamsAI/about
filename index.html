About

The story behind the curiosity-driven development.

I've been fascinated by artificial intelligence since I was a kid. Back in 2003, before I even knew what "machine learning" meant, I built my first AI prototype — a simple pattern recognition system that could barely distinguish between basic shapes. It was crude, but it worked, and more importantly, it sparked a curiosity that has never stopped growing.

The Journey

Over the past two decades, I've evolved from curious tinkerer to building production-ready AI systems. My work spans voice recognition, text-to-speech synthesis, home automation, and natural language processing — always with a focus on privacy-first, local-first architectures.

Current Projects

Right now I'm deep into GoogolPlex 2025 — a modern take on the legendary 2014 iOS Siri hack, but rebuilt as an open-source, extensible framework. I'm also working on the Luxa Voice Suite for Home Assistant integration and various speech synthesis projects.

Philosophy

I believe the most powerful AI should run on your own devices. That's why I build with C#, Python, and focus on offline-capable architectures. When your AI assistant runs locally, you maintain control over your data, your conversations, and your digital autonomy.
